Clearance Promises

With the basic implementation of FLAP as largely discussed on this site and as currently modelled, there is no means of of being sure, om completion of one trip, when you will be allowed to start the next. This is because the amount credited to each "grounded" Traveller, that is each Traveller with a distance balance in deficit, varies over time according to the size of the Daily Total and the number of grounded Travellers.

"Clearance Promises" are a simple extension to provide such certainty. They enable Travellers (in practice more likely air lines on behalf of Travellers), to submit flight details for a trip to FLAP at the point when they are booked rather than at check-in. In return FLAP creates a "Clearance Promise" for the Traveller: a fixed date after the completion of the trip when the Traveller can fly again, regardless of their distance balance.

The key to Clearance Promises is the devising of an algorithm that calculates accurate clearance dates - dates that are on average consistent with when the distance balance returns to credit. This will be a core goal of the next iteration of the implementation.

In Practice

With Clearance Promises in place most Travellers will be entirely insulated from the details of how FLAP works. Their experience will experience instead will simply be via a calendar, very similar to a calendar presented on-line for the booking of holiday accomodation, with each day colour coded according to its currrent FLAP state:

  • Travelling - on a trip
  • Cleared - free to travel
  • Grounded - not allowed to travel

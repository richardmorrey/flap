FLying is A Privilege

So what is this all about then?

It is all about FLAP, a non-judgemental system/scheme for reducing global air travel to help save the planet

Do we really need to reduce air travel? I've heard it only accounts for 2% of global emissiones after all. Surely carbon-offsetting/biofuels/electric planes will save us?

Sadly not no. Take a look at Figures 8.1 (p606) and 8.3 (p608) in ICC 2018 Report Chapter 8 to get a better idea of air travel's significant and, critically, increasing contribution to global CO2 emissions. This short article by United Kingdom Green Party MP Caroline Lucas explains the reasons why air travel reduction is the only way to address this in time. Finally this article, by George Monbiot, also short, explains why biofuels in particular are such a very bad idea.

Blimey I havent got time to read all that stuff. Just tell me about FLAP so I can get on with my day

Sure. It is pretty simple really - a scheme/on-line computer system for directly managing the total distance travelled by air each day. It works as follows:

  • Every time someone checks in for a flight the airline reports the details of the flight to FLAP. Airlines are legally obliged to do this.
  • FLAP maintains a "distance account", similar to a bank account, for every travller, with a balance of distance travlled by air.
  • Every time a flight check-in is reported FLAP debits the distance of that flight from the traveller's balance.
  • A single value, the Daily Total, specifies the total distance that can be travelled by air each day.
  • Each day FLAP credits all distance accounts in deficit with an equal share of the Daily Total.
  • If a traveller attempts to start a new trip when in a negative balance FLAP instructs the airline to refuse the check-in.Airlines are legally obliged to do this.
